📊 Key Analytical Insight: The R² vs. Directional Paradox

While Tree-based models (LightGBM, XGBoost) achieved near-perfect R² scores (~0.98), their Directional Accuracy remained stagnant at ~53% (barely above a 50% random coin flip).


Why? By strictly shifting features (e.g., SMA_10) to prevent data leakage, the models were forced to learn from yesterday's data. The models successfully learned Market Inertia (i.e., the market will stay around 22,000 tomorrow if it is at 22,000 today), resulting in low point-error (High R²). However, the exact daily noise (whether it goes up by 20 points or down by 20 points) is fundamentally unpredictable using only historical price indicators.
